## Project 3: Industrial Equipment Health-Monitoring Concept

### Problem

Industrial equipment reliability efforts can become reactive when teams are primarily responding to field failures after symptoms appear. Mining trucks and heavy equipment often contain numerous onboard sensors, but those signals may not be fully used for proactive health monitoring, trend analysis, or preventive alerting.

### Solution Concept

Developed an early-stage concept for real-time health monitoring of mining-truck electrical control systems using onboard sensor data, edge-device communication, and local mining-site wireless infrastructure.

The goal was to explore how existing control-system signals and site communications could support proactive reliability monitoring. Instead of relying only on reactive field-issue response, the system concept would collect equipment health data, identify concerning trends, support preventive alerts, and provide reliability teams with better information before component failures occur.

### Key Capabilities Proposed

* Onboard electrical control system health monitoring
* Sensor-data collection from existing control-system signals
* Edge-device communication concept
* Use of mining-site wireless infrastructure
* Preventive alerting concept
* Component-failure risk detection
* Trend analysis and reliability data collection
* Remote visibility for engineering and reliability teams

### Skills Demonstrated

* Systems engineering
* Industrial equipment reliability
* Electric controls understanding
* Sensor-data opportunity identification
* Edge-system concept development
* Reliability workflow design
* Technical translation between field problems and software-enabled solutions
* Cross-functional concept handoff

### Status

Early-stage concept development completed. Concept was handed off to dedicated software engineering resources for further evaluation and development.

## Additional Technical Background

### Independent Sound Localization Research

Built a microphone-grid concept using miniature microphone sensors and Arduino UNO processor boards. Processed microphone-grid data from Arduino into MATLAB for plotting and analysis, then evaluated sound source location through regression analysis.

### Microprocessor Communication Bus Research

Explored communication bus concepts for microprocessors with varying reference voltages. Studied UART, I2C, and SPI protocols and experimented with voltage-limiting concepts using parallel bus lines to adjust communication voltage levels.
